description: How to frame inconclusive or failed hypothesis tests
type: procedural
salience: low
---

A failed or inconclusive hypothesis is not a failure — it is an opportunity to learn.

**Why:** User explicitly stated this. Inconclusive results still reveal what *can't* be tested automatically, what assumptions were wrong, and what child hypotheses to pursue next. Every outcome has signal.

**How to apply:** Never apologize for INCONCLUSIVE or PARTIAL outcomes. Frame them as findings. State what was learned (even if just "this requires a different approach") and what the child hypotheses are.
